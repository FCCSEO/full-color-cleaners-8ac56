Frequently Asked Questions About House Soft Washing

How is soft washing different from pressure washing, and which is better for my house?
Soft washing and pressure washing are different techniques we use depending on the surface. Soft washing uses lower pressure and specialized cleaning solutions to safely remove dirt, algae, and mold from delicate surfaces like vinyl siding and stucco. Pressure washing uses higher pressure and is better for tough surfaces like concrete and driveways. At Full Color Cleaners, we assess your home's exterior materials to determine the most appropriate and safe cleaning method to prevent damage while achieving the best results.
How often should I have my gutters cleaned in Austin?
In Austin, we recommend having your gutters cleaned at least twice per year due to our heavy oak tree pollen in spring and leaf fall in autumn. However, if you have many trees near your home, you might need more frequent cleaning. Clogged gutters can lead to water damage and foundation issues, especially during our intense Texas rain storms. Full Color Cleaners can assess your specific situation and recommend an appropriate maintenance schedule.
Do you offer window cleaning for multi-story homes?
Yes, Full Color Cleaners provides professional window cleaning services for both single and multi-story homes in Austin. Our team has the proper equipment and experience to safely clean windows at height, including both interior and exterior surfaces. We ensure streak-free results while maintaining safety protocols.
When should I schedule Christmas light installation?
For Christmas light installation in Austin, we recommend booking at least 4-6 weeks before you want your lights displayed. Our schedule fills up quickly during the holiday season, especially in November. Contact us for a free quote and to secure your preferred installation date. We handle everything from installation to maintenance during the season, and removal after the holidays.
What's included in your roof cleaning service?
Our roof cleaning service removes black streaks, moss, algae, and other organic growth that can damage your roof over time. The process includes a thorough inspection, careful cleaning of all roof surfaces, and cleaning of any runoff from gutters and surrounding areas.
